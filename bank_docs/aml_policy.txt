Bank Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Policy

1. CASH TRANSACTION REPORTING
All cash transactions exceeding $10,000 CAD must be 
reported to FINTRAC within 15 business days.
Multiple transactions by same customer totaling 
$10,000 in 24 hours must also be reported.

2. SUSPICIOUS TRANSACTION REPORTING
Any transaction suspected of being related to money 
laundering must be reported immediately.
Employees must not tip off customers about reports.
Reports must be filed within 30 days of suspicion.

3. CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE
All new customers must provide government issued ID.
High risk customers require enhanced due diligence.
PEP (Politically Exposed Persons) require additional 
screening and senior management approval.

4. RECORD KEEPING
All transaction records must be kept for 7 years.
Customer identification records kept for 7 years 
after account closure.
Records must be available to FINTRAC within 30 days.

5. EMPLOYEE TRAINING
All employees must complete AML training annually.
New employees must complete within 30 days of joining.
Training records must be maintained for 5 years.
